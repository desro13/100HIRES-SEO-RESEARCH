# 100Hires SEO Research — AI-Powered SEO Content Production

A comprehensive portfolio research project submitted for the 100Hires application. This project investigates how AI is reshaping search engine optimization through original research, expert curation, and transcript analysis.

## Project Overview

The shift from traditional search to AI-powered discovery (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini) is fundamentally changing how content gets found, cited, and ranked. This research examines the strategies, frameworks, and tools that practitioners are using to navigate this transition.

### Scope

- **10 experts curated** from genuine, active practitioners in AI-driven SEO
- **30+ video transcripts** analyzed across YouTube content
- **10 LinkedIn profiles** documented with recent post themes
- **5 major frameworks** identified for AI-powered SEO

## Key Findings

### 1. AEO / GEO / LLMO — The New Optimization Layer
Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) are emerging as distinct disciplines alongside traditional SEO. Experts agree that optimizing for AI-generated answers (LLMO) requires different tactics than ranking on Google SERPs.

### 2. Freshness Drives AI Citations
Ahrefs research found that **76% of AI-cited pages were updated within 30 days**. Content freshness is the single strongest predictor of AI citation — more important than domain authority or backlink count in several studies.

### 3. Entity Depth Over Keyword Density
AI search engines favor topical depth and entity relationships over keyword matching. Content that thoroughly covers an entity (person, place, concept) with context and structure outperforms keyword-optimized surface-level content in AI-generated answers.

### 4. AI Agents for Automated SEO Workflows
Multiple experts (Greg Isenberg, Eric Siu, Tim the SEO Guru) demonstrate AI agent loops that automate content production, citation monitoring, and ranking optimization — reducing manual SEO work significantly while maintaining or improving results.

### 5. E-E-A-T Signals in the AI Era
Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) framework remains critical, but its application has evolved. AI citations increasingly favor content with clear authorship, cited sources, factual accuracy, and demonstrated expertise — making E-E-A-T optimization more measurable than ever.

## Methodology

1. **Expert selection**: Practitioners were verified for active YouTube presence (video uploads within 30 days) and LinkedIn activity.
2. **Transcript collection**: Video transcripts were collected and analyzed for recurring themes, frameworks, and tactical recommendations.
3. **LinkedIn research**: Each expert's LinkedIn profile was reviewed for recent post themes and engagement patterns.
4. **Framework synthesis**: Recurring patterns across all 10 experts were distilled into the five key findings above.

This repository is designed to support the creation of a practical AI SEO playbook. The transcripts provide tactical details, the LinkedIn profiles show community engagement patterns, and the key findings serve as strategic pillars.
