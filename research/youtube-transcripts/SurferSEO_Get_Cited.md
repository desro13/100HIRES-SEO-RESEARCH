## Summary

Matt Kenyon's comprehensive strategy for getting cited across all major AI search platforms — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini.

## Key Points

- **Step 1: Understand AI Search Mechanics**: AI search uses retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) — it retrieves relevant content and generates answers. Optimizing for retrieval is different from optimizing for ranking.
- **Step 2: Content Structure for AI**: Format content for AI extraction — clear headings, bullet points for key facts, concise definitions, and structured data. Think of your content as an API for AI assistants.
- **Step 3: The AI Tracker Tool**: Monitor brand mentions across LLMs to measure what's working. Without measurement, optimization is guesswork.
- **Step 4: Technical Foundation**: Ensure AI crawlers can access your content — no paywalls blocking GPTBot, clear robots.txt rules, proper sitemaps.
- **Step 5: Ongoing Optimization**: AI optimization isn't one-time. As AI models update and citation patterns change, continuous monitoring and adjustment is necessary.

## Practical Takeaways

- Format content as "AI-ready" with clear extraction points
- Use LLM monitoring tools to track AI citations
- Ensure technical accessibility for AI crawlers
- Treat AI optimization as an ongoing process, not a one-time project
