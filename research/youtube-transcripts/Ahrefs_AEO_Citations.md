# Ahrefs_AEO_Citations

**Expert:** Ahrefs (Sam Oh)
**Video Topic:** AEO Citations — How to Get Cited in AI Search Results
**Source:** youtube.com/@AhrefsCom

## Summary

Sam Oh presents Ahrefs' proprietary research on AI citation patterns, drawing from a 75K-brand study and analysis of 55.8 million AI Overview results. The video provides data-backed answers to what drives AI citations.

## Key Points

- **The 75K Brand Study**: Ahrefs analyzed 75,000 brands across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews. Key finding: only 21% of brands appear in AI results at all, creating a massive opportunity for the other 79%.
- **Freshness is King**: 76% of AI-cited pages were updated or published within the last 30 days. Content older than 90 days has a 90%+ drop-off rate in AI citations.
- **Unique Sources Per Assistant**: 86% of top-cited sources are unique to each AI assistant. This means brands need different strategies for ChatGPT vs. Perplexity vs. Gemini.
- **AI Referral Traffic**: AI referral traffic at Ahrefs converts 9x higher than organic search traffic. The quality bar is higher — AI cites content it "trusts."
- **Query Fan-Out**: AI engines don't just answer one search query — they fan out across related concepts. Content that covers a topic comprehensively performs better than page-level optimization.

## Practical Takeaways

- Prioritize freshness: update content regularly, ideally every 30 days
- Optimize for each AI assistant separately (don't use a one-size-fits-all approach)
- Focus on building trust signals (E-E-A-T) — AI referral traffic is high-intent
- Create comprehensive topic clusters, not individual keyword-targeted pages
