# NathanGotch_ChatGPT_SEO

**Expert:** Nathan Gotch
**Video Topic:** ChatGPT SEO — How to Rank in ChatGPT Answers
**Source:** youtube.com/@nathangotch

## Summary

Gotch breaks down the mechanics of how ChatGPT selects sources and how content creators can optimize for inclusion in ChatGPT's responses. He covers both the training data and real-time retrieval aspects of ChatGPT's citation system.

## Key Points

- **Real-Time Retrieval Dominance**: Most ChatGPT citations come from real-time web retrieval (browsing), not training data. This means optimization is about being findable at query time, not getting into training data.
- **Source Diversity**: ChatGPT cites differently than Google. 86% of top-cited sources are unique to each AI assistant — meaning optimizing for ChatGPT alone misses Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude.
- **Formatting Matters**: ChatGPT prefers content with clear hierarchical structure. H2/H3 headers that read as complete questions get cited more frequently.
- **"Fan-Out" Queries**: ChatGPT doesn't answer one keyword — it fans out across multiple interpretations of a query. Content should cover the full context around a topic, not just a specific keyword.
- **Recency Bias**: ChatGPT shows a strong recency bias in browsing mode. Pages updated within the past 7–14 days are significantly more likely to be cited.

## Practical Takeaways

- Update content frequently, ideally every 7–14 days
- Use question-format headers (H2s that are actual questions people ask)
- Cover the full "question ecosystem" around a topic, not just one keyword
- Don't optimize exclusively for ChatGPT — optimize for multi-platform AI discovery
