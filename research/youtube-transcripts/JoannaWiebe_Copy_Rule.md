## Summary

Joanna Wiebe breaks down the single most important principle in copywriting — specificity — and how it applies in the age of AI-generated content.

## Key Points

- **Specificity is the #1 Rule**: Generic writing gets ignored. Specific claims, specific numbers, specific examples — these are what capture attention and build trust.
- **AI's Weakness**: AI generates generic, averaged content by default. The human advantage is specificity — personal experience, unique data, original insights, and real examples.
- **The Specificity-Authority Connection**: Google's E-E-A-T and AI citation patterns both reward specific, verifiable claims over generic statements. Specific content builds authority.
- **How to Be Specific**: Use numbers (not "many" but "76%"), name names (not "a study" but "Ahrefs' 2026 study"), share personal experience (not "businesses find" but "we found").

## Practical Takeaways

- Default to specific over generic in every sentence
- Use the "specificity audit" — scan content and replace general claims with specifics
- Train AI prompts to request specific data and examples
- Personal experience is your moat against AI commoditization of content
